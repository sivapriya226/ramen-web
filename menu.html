Tonkotsu Ramen

Originating from Fukuoka, Japan, this ramen features a rich and creamy pork bone broth. It's often garnished with chashu pork, wood ear mushrooms, pickled ginger, and may include additional toppings like sesame seeds or spicy mustard greens.

Shoyu Ramen

A classic Japanese ramen with a clear, savory broth beautifully seasoned with soy sauce. Typically topped with slices of tender roast pork (chashu), crunchy bamboo shoots, fresh green onions, flavorful nori, and a perfectly soft-boiled egg.

Vegan Ramen

A meat-free option that still bursts with flavor, typically featuring a vegetable or mushroom-based broth. Toppings can vary widely but commonly include tofu, assorted vegetables, along with nori and a soft-boiled egg for added richness.

Miso Ramen

Hailing from Hokkaido, Japan, miso ramen boasts a flavorful broth made from fermented soybean paste (miso). It's hearty and often includes toppings such as corn, butter, bean sprouts, ground pork, and garlic oil.

Hiyashi Chuka

Cold ramen dish popular in Japan during the hot summer months. It features chilled noodles topped with various colorful toppings such as thinly sliced omelet, ham, cucumber, tomato, and sometimes crab or shrimp.

Tantanmen Ramen

A fiery variation of ramen featuring a spicy broth usually made with chili oil or spicy miso paste. Toppings can include sliced pork, bean sprouts, cabbage, carrots, and sometimes even spicy ground pork.
